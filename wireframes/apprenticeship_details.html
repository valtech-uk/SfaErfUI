Paralegal apprenticeship

Download full details (PDF, 268KB)

What does a paralegal do?

 

Paralegals provide support to senior legal staff.

 

They may be asked to:

 

• manage records

• carry out research

• draft, produce and review legal documents

• handle confidential information

• understand and use basic financial information

• meet with clients

 

What skills do paralegals need?

 

paralegals need to be comfortable:

 

• working with numbers

• meeting tight deadlines

• earning to use software quickly

• thinking logically

• dealing with clients

• paying attention to details

 

What does the apprenticeship involve?

 

Apprentices work with teams of actuaries to build mathematical models.

 

The programme is built around on-the-job training, but also involves studying for professional exams. It’s the start of a career in the industry.

 

 

      Apprenticeship pay and holidays.

 

 

What are the entry requirements?

 

Employers can choose their own entry requirements.

 

What skills will apprentices learn?

 

Apprentices will learn skills including:

 

• how actuarial software, technology and tools can be used

• the structure of the financial services market

• the industry’s professional requirements

 

 

      For a full list download the standard (PDF, 268KB).

 

 

What are the English and maths requirements?

 

Apprentices must have passed Level 2 English and maths before their final assessment, if they don’t have the qualifications already.

 

How long does the apprenticeship last?

 

The apprenticeship usually takes 2-3 years.

 

What qualifications does the apprenticeship lead to?

 

This is a level 3 apprenticeship.
