How to employ an apprentice

What are your responsibilities as an employer?

When you employ an apprentice, you’re agreeing to provide:

 

• meaningful work

• a salary

• time to train

• on-the-job training

• an employment contract

• an apprenticeship agreement

• a statement of commitment

 

Your apprentice could be new to workplaces. You may need to show them what’s expected of employees.

 

Pay and conditions

 

You must pay your apprentice at least £2.73 an hour. Most employers choose to pay more than this.

 

If they’re 19 or older and have completed the first year of their apprenticeship, they’re entitled to national minimum wage.

 

Training

 

Apprentices need paid time off for training.

 

This includes any extra time for English and maths study, or support if they have disabilities or learning difficulties.

 

Hours

 

Apprentices usually spend:

 

• 30 hours a week working

• one day a week training

 

They must work for at least 16 hours a week, and be able to complete their apprenticeships inside their contracted hours.

 

Ending the apprenticeship early

 

Your apprentice is an employee with a contract. You can't usually make them redundant just because you can't afford to pay them, for example.

 

If you want to end the apprenticeship early for another reason you should get legal advice.
