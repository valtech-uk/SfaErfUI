Employ an apprentice

Hiring apprentices helps you build a workforce that’s motivated, skilled and qualified. Use this service to choose an apprenticeship and find out about funding.

Estimate your funding

How hiring works

There are six steps to taking on an apprentice:

  1. Choose an apprenticeship standard

    A standard is a list of the skills that apprentices will have once they’ve completed their apprenticeships.

    Every job role has a different standard.

  2. Apply for funding

    The government will partly fund your apprenticeship.

    The amount of funding depends on the apprentice’s age and the size of your business, among other things.

  3. Find a training provider

    Training providers are colleges or other organisations that carry out your apprentice’s training.

    Some providers train apprentices at a campus, others will travel to you.

  4. Recruit your apprentice

    Apprentices can be someone you already know, or someone new.

    Depending on factors like which standard you choose and where you’re based, your apprentice could join you within a few weeks.

  5. Start the apprenticeship

    As an employer you’ll have to agree to some simple terms and conditions.

    They include asking your apprentice to sign an agreement, and meeting national minimum wage laws.

  6. Complete the apprenticeship

    Apprentices must be assessed during their programme.

    Your training provider will find an assessment organisation for you, or you can look for one yourself.
